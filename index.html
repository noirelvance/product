Before You Start

This guide strips away everything that doesn't directly lead to a sale. No business plans. No logo design. No "finding your passion." Just seven focused days of execution.

Your first sale isn't about money. It's about proof. Proof that someone will exchange their money for something you created. Once you have that proof, everything becomes easier.

Each day has one task. Complete it before moving on. Momentum matters more than perfection.

01

Choose Something People Already Buy

Do not invent. Do not innovate. Your goal is to find a problem people are already paying to solve, then solve it in your own way. The market has already validated the demand—you just need to meet it.

Key Insight

If someone is selling a similar product and making money, that's not competition—that's validation.

Spend today researching what's already selling. Check Gumroad's trending products. Browse Etsy's bestsellers. Look at what courses are popular on Udemy. Notice what problems keep appearing.

Proven Product Categories
  • Templates that save time (Notion, spreadsheets, documents)
  • Guides that shortcut learning (how-to guides, checklists, frameworks)
  • Tools that solve specific problems (calculators, trackers, planners)
  • Resources for specific professions (designers, developers, marketers)
Today's Deliverable

Write one sentence: "I help [specific person] achieve [specific outcome]."

02

Create Your Product in 3 Hours or Less

Your first product should be embarrassingly simple. Not because simple is bad, but because complexity is the enemy of shipping. The goal is a working version, not a perfect one.

Key Insight

A finished simple product beats an unfinished complex one. Every single time.

Set a timer for 3 hours. Create something that delivers real value but can be completed in that window. If you can't finish in 3 hours, you've scoped too big. Cut ruthlessly.

3-Hour Product Ideas
  • A 10-page PDF guide solving one specific problem
  • A Notion template with clear instructions
  • A spreadsheet that automates a tedious calculation
  • A checklist that prevents common mistakes
  • A 30-minute video walkthrough of your expertise
Does it solve a real problem someone has right now?
Can someone use it immediately after purchase?
Would you pay for this if you had the problem it solves?
Today's Deliverable

A complete (not perfect) first version of your product, saved and ready to deliver.

03

Make It Possible to Pay You

You don't need a website. You don't need a brand. You need one page with one button. That's it. Your sales page has exactly one job: make it clear what someone gets and let them pay.

Key Insight

You're not convincing anyone. You're allowing people who already want this to buy it.

Use Gumroad, Lemonsqueezy, or Stripe Payment Links. All three take less than 20 minutes to set up. Your page needs only these elements:

Headline: What transformation do they get?
Subheadline: Who is this for and what's included?
3-5 Bullets: Specific benefits they'll receive
Price + Button: Make it obvious and clickable
Today's Deliverable

A live page where someone can click a button and pay you money.

04

Price for Momentum, Not Profit

Your first sale is not about making money. It's about breaking through the psychological barrier of someone paying you for something you created. Price accordingly.

Key Insight

A $9 sale teaches you the same lessons as a $99 sale, but happens 10x faster.

Choose a price so low that saying "yes" requires almost no thought. The sweet spot for first products: $5-$19. This isn't about undervaluing yourself. It's about removing friction from your first transaction.

Pricing Psychology
  • $5-9: Impulse buy territory. Nearly zero friction.
  • $10-19: Still low-risk, but feels more substantial.
  • $20-49: Requires a bit more consideration.
  • $50+: Save this for after you have proof and testimonials.

After your first 10 sales, raise the price. Then raise it again after 25. Let the market tell you what it's worth. But first, get that initial momentum.

Today's Deliverable

Set your price between $5-$19. Update your payment page. Done.

05

Put It in Front of Real People

Today is uncomfortable. Today you share your work publicly. Not to convince anyone, not to pitch aggressively—just to be visible. You cannot make a sale if no one knows you're selling.

Key Insight

One genuine post to 100 people beats a perfect pitch to zero people.

Share once in each place where your potential customers already spend time. Don't over-explain. Don't apologize. State what you made, who it's for, and include the link.

Where to Share
  • Twitter/X: "I made [thing] for [people]. [Link]"
  • LinkedIn: Share the problem you solve with a brief story
  • Reddit: Find the relevant subreddit, follow their rules
  • Communities: Slack groups, Discord servers, Facebook groups
  • Direct: DM 10 people who might genuinely benefit
Post publicly in at least 2 places
Send direct messages to 10 relevant people
Tell 5 friends who might know someone interested
Today's Deliverable

Your link shared publicly + 10 direct messages sent to potential buyers.

06

Improve One Thing Based on Feedback

By now you've gotten signals. Page visits. Questions. Maybe objections. Today you make exactly one improvement based on what you've learned. Not a redesign. Not a pivot. One adjustment.

Key Insight

Small iterations compound. One 10% improvement per week = 14x better in a year.

Look at the data you have: Did people click but not buy? Clarity problem. Did people not click? Headline problem. Did people ask questions? Missing information problem.

Common First Fixes
  • Weak headline: Make the benefit more specific and immediate
  • Unclear value: Add a "What you get" section with specifics
  • Trust gap: Add a simple guarantee or your credentials
  • Price confusion: Make the price more prominent
  • No urgency: Add a reason to buy today
Today's Deliverable

One specific change to your sales page based on real feedback or data.

07

First Sale or First Data—Both Are Wins

Today you assess. Either you made a sale—which proves demand exists—or you collected data that tells you exactly what to adjust. Both outcomes move you forward. Only inaction fails.

Key Insight

The person who made $7 this week knows more than the person who spent 6 months planning.

If you made a sale:

Reach out to your buyer. Ask what convinced them to purchase.
Ask for a testimonial. Use their words in your marketing.
Plan how to get 10 more sales using what worked.

If you didn't make a sale:

Review your traffic. Did enough people see it?
Ask 3 people why they didn't buy. Listen without defending.
Identify the biggest gap: audience, offer, or clarity?
Today's Deliverable

Document what happened and your one clear next step based on the results.
